Inference in hard-to-reach populations

I am a leader of a CNPq group on this theme, where we mainly try to answer two questions about hard to reach populations (i) How many are they? (ii) Who are they? The first question we explore a method called Network Scale-up (NSUM); The second we deal with data from time location sampling (TLS) and Respondent-driven sampling (RDS) designs.

Modelling infectious diseases

I am involved in modelling and analysis of infectious diseases data. I have been working on several type of infectious, sexually transmitted infections such as HIV, syphilis, and hepatitis, mosquito-borne infectious diseases like Malaria, and the arboviroses dengue, Zika, and chikungunya. Our interesting relies on describe using a model-based approach the disease epidemiology via providing prevalences, incidences, and risk factors, and also we are interested on provide early warning models that may be able to antecipate an large epideic, or even an small outbreak.

Projects:

  • Space-time epidemiology of arboviroses
  • e-Surveillance project is a project for statistical and mathematical methods for disease surveillance.
    • Infodengue is a warning system with cases of dengue in the 790 (on August 2018) Brazilian municipalities. (link)
    • Infogripe is a warning system for Severe acute respiratory syndrome in Brazil. (link)

Projects:

  • Divas project is a Respondent-driven sampling study aiming to learn about the individual and overall reality of Transsexual and Transvestite women in Brazil. (link)
  • Pesquisa Nacional do Crack is a National survey on Crack-cocaine users. This study has two components: (i) TLS component to sample crack users; and (ii) NSUM survey aiming to estimated the number of crack users in the 27 Brazilian capitals.
