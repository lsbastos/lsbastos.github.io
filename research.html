Inference in hard-to-reach populations

I am a leader of a CNPq group on this theme, where we mainly try to answer two questions about hard to reach populations (i) How many are they? (ii) Who are they? The first question we explore a method called Network Scale-up (NSUM); The second we deal with data from time location sampling (TLS) and Respondent-driven sampling (RDS) designs.

Projects:

  • Divas project is a Respondent-driven sampling study aiming to learn about the individual and overall reality of Transsexual and Transvestite women in Brazil. (link)
  • Pesquisa Nacional do Crack is a National survey on Crack-cocaine users. This study has two components: (i) TLS component to sample crack users; and (ii) NSUM survey aiming to estimated the number of crack users in the 27 Brazilian capitals.
