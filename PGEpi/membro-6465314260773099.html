Pesquisa no Programa de Epidemiologia em Saude Publica da ENSP

Marilia Sa Carvalho

Graduada em Medicina pela Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro (1979), com mestrado em Saúde Pública pela Fundação Oswaldo Cruz (1990), doutorado em Engenharia Biomédica pela Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro (1997) e pós-doutorado em estatística na Universidade de Lancaster. Pesquisadora titular da Fundação Oswaldo Cruz, orienta no programa de pós-graduação "Epidemiologia em Saúde Pública". As principais áreas de interesse são epidemiologia ambiental, os efeitos do clima na saúde e as doenças transmissíveis, em especial as arboviroses. Recentemente vem procurando incorporar ao conjunto de projetos nos quais participa uma abordagem baseada no pensamento sistêmico. É editora associada da PLOS Neglected Tropical Diseases e desde 2013 exerce a função de editora-chefe do Cadernos de Saúde Pública. (Texto informado pelo autor)

                                          8. 2013-2014. An integrated approach for understanding dengue epidemiology and disease
                                            Descrição: Dengue is a complex disease and although many of the key factors implicated in dengue epidemiology are qualitatively known, the way they interplay is not clearly understood and there are several fundamental lacunae. In order to understand the complex interplay among viral, human, mosquito and environmental factors in dengue virus (DENV) transmission and development of severe dengue disease, we will develop a multidisciplinary project based on two existing cohorts, in Brazil and Cambodia, to bring in expertise from different disciplines in dengue research. The objectives of this project are (i) to develop a dengue fever theoretical epidemiology framework by systematic review of retrospective data, thereby enable elaboration of a refined protocol for prospective multi-site study of dengue epidemiology; (ii) perform pilot sero-prevalence studies in the Brazil cohort using novel antigens and (iii) exchange resources, technical know-how and biological products to enable the development of a holistic approach to the analysis of key factors determining dengue epidemiology and infection outcome. We will combine a retrospective collation of information on dengue epidemiology with prospective pilot sero-prevalence studies. Fiocruz is currently running a dengue cohort that can provide biological samples for evaluation of a novel sero-prevalence ELISA developed at Institut Pasteur. In addition, the cohorts in Brazil and Cambodia can isolate viruses that can then be tested with respect to infectivity and growth in human and mosquito cell cultures..
